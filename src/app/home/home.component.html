Indian Railways is a state-owned enterprise that is organized as a departmental undertaking of the Ministry of Railways of the Government of India and operates India's national railway system. As of 2024, it manages the fourth largest national railway system by size with a track length of 135,207 km (84,014 mi), running track length of 109,748 km (68,194 mi), and route length of 69,181 km (42,987 mi).

Michigan State University seeks participants for railway management programme

APPLICATIONS remain open for Michigan State University’s Railway Management Certificate Programme, which is aimed at individuals working in the railway industry who have the potential to become senior managers within their organisations.

The programme consists of four week-long residential modules, covering both freight and passenger operations, and is designed to provide broad experience of the diversity and complexity of today’s North American railway industry.

The programme’s largest-ever cohort of 30 rail professionals completed the course in 2024, the 19th time it has been run. MSU’s railway education programme director, Valerii Kucherenko, is hoping for a similarly strong attendance for the 20th edition in 2025.

The Role of Surface Treatments in Protecting Railway Components

Through its experts and “special process” auditors, TRIGO Aerospace, Defence Rail provides inspection solutions for the various surface treatment processes implemented to protect railway components against corrosion and wear. These treatments include galvanization, powder coatings, and anodic treatments, which are essential for maintaining the integrity of parts exposed to harsh environmental conditions. Additionally, our “Painting, Coating, Anodizing, Galvanization” experts are well-versed in industry standards and those deployed by major train manufacturers

TRIGO Aerospace, Defence Rail offers rigorous quality control services to ensure that railway components meet the highest safety and performance standards.
